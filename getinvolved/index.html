Controlling Parrotfeather

Physical removal of parrotfeather causes increased spread of the plant. On top of this, a waxy coating on the plant causes herbicides and other chemicals to be ineffective in removing the plant. While the only reliable way to remove a population so far is draining the body of water it lives in, research is being done to find a more efficient way to control it.


What You Can Do

The most important thing you can do to help stop the spread of parrotfeather is not purchase it for your garden ponds to help slow the spread of it to your area. You can also spread the word to your friends and family, asking them to do the same. If you currently have a pond with parrotfeather, you can drain it to let the parrotfeather die. If doing so is impossible, physical removal of parrotfeather can be effective in a closed-off system such as a garden pond as long as the roots are fully raked out.
